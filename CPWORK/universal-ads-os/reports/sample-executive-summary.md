## Are We Profitable on Paid Advertising?

**Yes.**

In May 2026, Example Co spent $7,841 on Meta and TikTok ads and generated $43,200 in attributed revenue. That is a 5.5x return on ad spend. With a 35% gross margin, the gross profit from those sales was $15,120. After deducting the $7,841 ad cost, the net contribution from paid advertising was **$7,279 profit**.

Paid advertising is working. We are running at nearly double the return needed to break even.

---

## What's Working

**1. The retargeting campaign is the engine.**
People who have already visited the website or engaged with a previous ad are converting at $30 per lead and $110 per sale. For every dollar spent on retargeting, the business gets back $22 in revenue. This is the strongest part of the account.

**2. Lead volume is growing.**
152 leads in May — up 29% from April (118 leads). Lead quality is averaging 70/100, which means roughly 70% of leads are considered genuinely interested prospects by the sales team.

**3. Meta is driving all the purchases.**
Meta Ads generated 18 purchases at an average sale value of $2,400. CPA was $284 — well below the $875 break-even. Sales team conversion rate from lead to purchase: 19% on Meta retargeting audiences.

**4. Spend is on budget.**
$7,841 of the $8,000 monthly budget was used — 98% efficiency. No waste.

---

## What's Broken (or Needs Attention)

**1. TikTok had a tracking problem for most of May.**
The TikTok purchase tracking pixel was broken from March until 22 May. This means we cannot reliably measure TikTok's return on investment for the past 2 months. TikTok has been generating leads cheaply ($47 per lead) but we cannot confirm how many sales those leads produced. This has been fixed and June data will be accurate. This also means TikTok's true contribution may be better than the numbers currently show.

**2. One Meta campaign was losing money.**
The "Broad Interest" campaign on Meta was spending $158 per lead with zero sales over 28 days. This was identified and paused on 28 May. No further budget will go to this campaign. The budget has been redirected to campaigns that are working.

**3. Some ad creatives are getting stale.**
Several ads have been running for 30+ days without replacement. When ads run too long, audiences start to ignore them — a pattern called creative fatigue. Two of Example Co's TikTok cold-audience ads are showing this pattern (lower engagement scores week-on-week). New creative assets are being developed and will replace these in June.
