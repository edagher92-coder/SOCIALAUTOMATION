## Recommendation

### Working Name: AdPilot OS

**Why it wins:**

1. **Scales across all four tiers and all audience segments.** A solo business owner can "pilot their own ads." A freelancer can "run their client's ad command centre." An agency can "white-label the OS." The metaphor holds at every price point.

2. **"OS" is the right technical framing.** This is not a single feature — it is a system of 12 agents, 25 skills, templates, dashboards, automations, and a config layer. "OS" communicates that accurately without requiring a technical explanation. Buyers understand that an OS is the layer everything else runs on.

3. **Numbers-first, anti-hype tone compatibility.** "AdPilot" implies control and navigation — not "10x results guaranteed." This aligns with the product's voice: practical, evidence-based, no magic promises.

4. **Domain and branding path is clean.** AdPilotOS.com, AdPilotOS.com.au, @AdPilotOS across socials — all likely available as of build date (verify before investing in brand assets). One clean word, one acronym.

5. **Abbreviates and sub-brands well.** "APO" as an internal shortcode. Tier sub-brands: "AdPilot Starter," "AdPilot Pro," "AdPilot Agency." Done-With-You service: "AdPilot Setup."

**Pre-launch checklist for this name:**
- [ ] Search IP Australia (ipaustralia.gov.au) for "AdPilot" in Class 35 (advertising, business services) and Class 42 (software as a service, computer services)
- [ ] Search USPTO if planning US expansion
- [ ] Verify domain availability: AdPilotOS.com, AdPilotOS.com.au
- [ ] Check social handles: @AdPilotOS on LinkedIn, Instagram, X/Twitter
- [ ] Google search "AdPilot" — note any similar products and assess confusion risk
- [ ] Register the business name / company if operating as a separate entity

---

### Runner-Up 1: Ad Command Centre

**Why second place:** Stronger for the agency and operator audience. "Command Centre" has authority, and the product already uses it as the name for the master routing agent (`start-ads-command-centre`). This makes it a natural choice if the primary buyer is agencies, not SMBs.

**Downside:** "Command Centre" feels slightly intimidating for the Starter tier (DIY business owners paying $97–297). Could use this as the Agency-tier sub-brand even if AdPilot OS wins as the master name.

**Suggested use:** Sub-brand for the Agency tier — "AdPilot Agency Command Centre" or "The Agency Command Centre by AdPilot OS."

---

### Runner-Up 2: Campaign Control OS

**Why third place:** Cleaner than MetaTik, less generic than "Performance Tracker," and the "Control" framing resonates with operators who feel they've lost visibility on their spend. "OS" suffix carries over from AdPilot OS.

**Downside:** "Campaign" narrows the scope. The product spans campaigns, creative, attribution, reporting, and automation. "Campaign Control" undersells it and could be confused with campaign management tools (e.g. campaign scheduling software). Suitable as a product description ("gain campaign control") but not as a primary name.

---

## Naming Architecture (recommended)

```
Master brand:        AdPilot OS
  Starter tier:      AdPilot Starter
  Pro tier:          AdPilot Pro
  Agency tier:       AdPilot Agency (or "Agency Command Centre")
  DWY service:       AdPilot Setup
  Internal router:   Ad Command Centre (agent name — keep as-is)
```

This gives each tier a distinct identity while maintaining brand coherence under AdPilot OS.
