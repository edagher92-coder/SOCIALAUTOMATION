## 2. Grant of Licence — What the Buyer Can Do

The Licensor grants to the Licensee a non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable licence to use the Licensed Materials in accordance with the terms of this document. The scope of that licence depends on the tier purchased.

### 2.1 Starter Licence (AUD $97–$297)

A Starter Licence holder may:

- Use all Licensed Materials included in the Starter tier — including Claude skill prompts, agent prompts, Google Sheets dashboard templates, CSV schemas, and associated SOP documents — for the purposes of managing and optimising their own business advertising accounts on Meta and TikTok.
- Run ad account audits, generate performance reports, and produce recommendations and health scores for their own accounts only.
- Adapt the `client-config.yaml` and other template files to reflect their own business name, account IDs, and campaign parameters.
- Use the UTM builder, creative matrix, and lead tracker tools for their own advertising campaigns.
- Share outputs produced by the system — including completed reports, audit documents, health scores, and written recommendations — with their own clients, employers, or stakeholders. Sharing outputs is not the same as sharing the source materials, and is permitted under this tier.

### 2.2 Pro Licence (AUD $497–$1,497)

A Pro Licence holder receives all rights granted under the Starter Licence, plus:

- Use of automation blueprints (Make, Zapier, and n8n workflow files) for their own advertising accounts or for a single employer or client engagement at a time.
- Operation of AdPilot OS in a freelancer capacity — that is, working for one client at a time under a service agreement — where the Licensee is the sole operator of the system on that client's behalf. This does not extend to concurrent commercial deployment across multiple paying clients at the same time.
- Delivery of reports, audits, health scores, and ad performance recommendations to a paying client as part of a defined service engagement.

### 2.3 Agency Licence (AUD $1,997–$4,997+)

An Agency Licence holder receives all rights granted under the Pro Licence, plus:

- The right to rebrand the AdPilot OS system under the Agency's own business name and product name (White-Label Rights), including replacing product branding in client-facing materials, reports, and dashboards.
- Deployment of the system across multiple concurrent client accounts, without restriction on the number of clients managed simultaneously.
- Delivery of the system as an ongoing managed service — for example, weekly or monthly reporting, health scoring, and ad audits — billed directly to end clients as part of the Agency's own service.
- The right to train the Agency's own employed or contracted team members to operate the system, provided those team members operate only under the supervision of and on behalf of the licensed Agency entity.
- Use of the included pitch deck templates, case study templates, and sales support materials for the purpose of selling the Agency's own services to prospective clients.
- Retention of all revenue earned from services delivered to clients using the AdPilot OS system. The Licensor makes no claim on revenue generated by the Agency through service delivery.

## 3. Restrictions by Tier

### 3.1 Restrictions Applying to All Tiers (Starter, Pro, and Agency)

The following are prohibited regardless of the tier purchased:

- **No resale or redistribution of source files.** You must not resell, redistribute, sublicense, or share the Licensed Materials themselves — including skill prompt files (.md), YAML configuration files, automation blueprints, SOP documents, or template files — with any person or business that has not purchased their own AdPilot OS licence.
- **No public upload.** You must not upload any Licensed Materials to any publicly accessible repository (including GitHub, GitLab, or similar), content sharing platform, file hosting service, or large language model training dataset.
- **No false attribution of the source materials.** You must not represent AdPilot OS source files as your own original creation when presenting or selling those source files to others. (Note: Agency holders may white-label the system for service delivery; this restriction applies to the source files themselves, not to service outputs.)
- **No policy-violating use.** You must not use the system to generate, test, review, or approve ad creative that violates Meta Advertising Standards or TikTok Advertising Policies.
- **No removal of safety controls.** You must not remove, disable, bypass, or alter any safety flags, paused-duplicate guardrails, live-edit blocks, or other protective controls built into the system. The system is designed to propose and create paused duplicates only — it does not edit live ads or delete anything. These controls must remain intact.
- **No unreviewed automatic changes to live accounts.** You must not use the system in a way that results in automatic, unreviewed changes being applied to live, actively spending advertising accounts.
- **No hardcoding credentials into shared files.** You must not circumvent the `{{client.*}}` variable pattern by hardcoding Meta API keys, TikTok API credentials, OAuth tokens, or other private credentials directly into Licensed Material files that may be shared, copied, or stored in shared environments.

### 3.2 Additional Restrictions for Starter and Pro Licences

In addition to all restrictions in Section 3.1, Starter and Pro Licence holders must not:

- Deploy the system commercially across multiple concurrent paying clients at the same time. Pro holders may work with one client at a time; Starter holders may not use the system for client work at all (outputs shared with clients excepted — see Section 2.1).
- Rebrand AdPilot OS and present it as their own product for the purpose of selling or licensing it to others.
- Sublicense or transfer the licence to another individual, contractor, or business entity.
- Provide the Licensed Materials — including skill prompt files, YAML configs, or automation blueprints — to end clients as a "DIY kit" for those clients to operate independently without the Licensee's involvement.

### 3.3 Specific Restrictions for Agency Licences

The following restrictions apply specifically to Agency Licence holders in relation to their White-Label Rights:

- White-Label Rights belong to the licensed Agency entity only. The Agency may not sub-license these rights to end clients or to partner agencies.
- The Agency may not resell the Licensed Materials as a standalone competing product. The Agency may deliver services using the system but must not hand over raw source files (prompts, blueprints, YAML configs) to clients as an independently operable product.
- White-label branding may only be applied by the licensed Agency entity. The Agency may not sub-contract the white-labelling itself to another agency or business that does not hold its own Agency Licence.
