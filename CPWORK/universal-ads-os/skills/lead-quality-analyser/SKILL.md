## Purpose
CPL is a vanity metric if the leads don't close. This skill connects ad-platform CPL data to CRM sales data to calculate the true cost per qualified lead, cost per appointment, cost per sale, and return on ad spend at the revenue level — then identifies which campaigns, ad sets, and creatives produce leads that actually buy.

## When to use
- "My CPL is $30 but nobody is buying — what's wrong?"
- "Analyse my lead quality across all campaigns"
- "Which campaign gives me the best-quality leads?"
- "Calculate my true cost per sale including lead drop-off"
- "I need to improve lead quality, not just get more leads"

## Inputs needed
- {{client.business_name}}, {{client.main_offer}}, {{client.average_sale_value}}, {{client.gross_margin}}
- {{client.currency}}, {{client.crm}}, {{client.conversion_events}}
- Ad-platform data: leads per campaign / ad set / ad, spend per campaign / ad set / ad, CPL per entity
- CRM data (export or manual input) for the same period:
  - Total leads received
  - Qualified leads (met minimum criteria — e.g. budget, location, decision-maker)
  - Appointments booked
  - Appointments attended (show rate)
  - Proposals sent
  - Sales / closed deals
  - Revenue from those deals
- Lead source field in CRM — must be populated per lead to attribute back to ad campaign (required for per-campaign analysis)
- Qualification criteria definition — what makes a lead "qualified" for {{client.business_name}}

## Workflow
1. **Establish lead quality funnel stages**: Define the stages for this client: Lead → Qualified Lead → Appointment Booked → Appointment Attended → Sale. Confirm which stages are tracked in {{client.crm}}.
2. **Compute funnel conversion rates**:
   - qualification_rate = qualified_leads / total_leads
   - appointment_rate = appointments_booked / qualified_leads
   - show_rate = appointments_attended / appointments_booked
   - close_rate = sales / appointments_attended
   - overall_lead_to_sale_rate = sales / total_leads
3. **Compute true cost metrics**:
   - cost_per_qualified_lead = spend / qualified_leads
   - cost_per_appointment = spend / appointments_attended
   - cost_per_sale = spend / sales (true CPA at revenue level)
   - revenue_per_lead = total_revenue / total_leads
   - true_roas = total_revenue / total_spend
4. **Compare to break-even**:
   - break_even_cpa = {{client.average_sale_value}} × {{client.gross_margin}}
   - break_even_roas = 1 / {{client.gross_margin}}
   - Flag if cost_per_sale > break_even_cpa — unprofitable at sale level even if CPL looks good
5. **Per-campaign lead quality breakdown** (if CRM lead source data is available):
   - For each campaign / ad set: compute qualification_rate, close_rate, cost_per_sale
   - Rank campaigns by cost_per_sale (lowest = best quality leads)
   - Flag campaigns with low CPL but high cost_per_sale as "cheap but poor quality"
   - Flag campaigns with higher CPL but low cost_per_sale as "expensive leads but better quality — consider increasing budget"
6. **Assign lead quality score per campaign** (0–10):
   - 10: close_rate ≥ client target AND cost_per_sale ≤ break_even_cpa × 0.7
   - 7–9: close_rate within 20% of target AND cost_per_sale ≤ break_even_cpa
   - 4–6: close_rate 20–50% below target OR cost_per_sale 100–130% of break_even_cpa
   - 1–3: close_rate >50% below target OR cost_per_sale >130% of break_even_cpa
   - 0: no sales data or all leads uncontactable
7. **Identify lead quality root causes**:
   - Low qualification rate → offer is attracting wrong audience OR lead form has insufficient qualification questions
   - Low show rate → speed-to-lead is too slow OR follow-up sequence is weak
   - Low close rate → sales process issue OR offer-market fit problem → route to offer-funnel-review
   - Low overall conversion with high volume → volume optimisation is overriding quality signals
8. **Populate lead-quality-scorecard.csv**: One row per campaign with all funnel metrics and lead quality score.
9. **Recommendations**: Prioritise by impact on cost_per_sale. Include: tighten qualification questions, change optimisation event, adjust audience, improve speed-to-lead, or pause low-quality campaigns.

## Safety rules
- live_edit_block: true — all recommendations are proposals; no live campaigns changed
- No individual lead names, email addresses, phone numbers, or personal data in output — aggregate only
- Do not recommend pausing campaigns based on lead quality alone without confirming sufficient data (≥15 qualified leads per campaign)
- Budget reallocation to higher-quality campaigns requires human "YES"
- Flag data confidence issues if CRM lead source is not populated — per-campaign analysis is invalid without source data

## Handoff rules
- If close rate is low despite good qualification rate, hand off to offer-funnel-review — the issue is in the sales process or offer, not the ads
- If qualification rate is low, hand off to creative-testing-lab to test tighter qualifying language in ad copy and lead forms
- If show rate is low, flag to titan-offer-funnel-strategist and {{client.crm}} automation review for speed-to-lead improvement
- Pass lead quality scores to campaign-health-monitor as input for the lead quality component (max 8 pts)
- Pass per-campaign quality scorecard to paid-ads-data-analysis if a full data analysis is running in parallel
